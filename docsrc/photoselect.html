photoselect

Calculate orientation-dependent photo-selection weight

Syntax
w = photoselect(tdmOri,labFrame,k,alpha)
Description

For photo-generated paramagnetic centers (such as photo-excited triplet states), photoselect calculates the photo-excitation selection weight w for a given light excitation geometry, molecular orientation, and transition dipole moment. w=0 indicates no excitation at all (E-field is perpendicular to transition dipole moment), and w=1 indicates full excitation (E-field parallel to transition dipole moment).

tdmOri specifies the orientation in the molecular frame of the electric transition dipole moment (tdm) of the the optical transition that is being excited. There are three ways to specify this orientation: (1) via a letter code, such as 'y', 'z', 'xy', etc.; (2) via a 3-element unit vector, such as [0;1;0]; and (3) as an array of the two spherical angles (in radians) [phi_tdm,theta_tdm]. For example, 'y', [0;1;0] and [pi/2 pi/2] all represents a tdm along the molecular y axis.

labFrame gives the three Euler angles (in radians) [phi theta chi] that describe the orientation of lab frame relative to the molecular frame. phi and theta determine the direction of the lab z axis (zL, aligned with the static magnetic field), chi additionally determines the direction of lab x and y (xL and yL).

kOri specifies the propagation direction of the excitation light beam in the laboratory frame, again either as a letter code, a 3-element vector, or a set of two spherical angles. For example, 'y' or [0;1;0] or [pi/2 pi/2] indicate propagation along the laboratory y axis (perpendicular to the static magnetic field), and 'z' or [0;0;1] or [0 0] indicate propagation along the laboratuory z axis (parallel to the static magnetic field).

alpha indicates the polarization angle, in radians. It determines the direction of the E-field vector in the plane perpendicular to the propagation direction.

Examples
tdm = 'xy';  % tdm along bisector of xy plane
ori = [0 0 0];  % lab frame aligned with molecular frame
k = 'y'; % propagation along lab y axis
alpha = pi/2;  % gives E-field parallel to B0
w = photoselect(tdm,ori,k,alpha)

ans =
    0.5000
